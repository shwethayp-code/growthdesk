# Design Samples — Pick Your v3 Direction

Four hero + nav + supporting-section mockups, each in a different visual style. Open each `.html` file in your browser, see how it feels, and pick one. The chosen direction will become the basis for `index-v3.html` (a third version of GrowthDesk alongside v1 futuristic and v2 editorial).

These are **not** full apps — each is a single landing-page section showing nav, hero, and one supporting strip. Enough to feel the design language. Once you pick one, the same tokens get applied across all 29 modules of the real app.

---

## Sample 1 — AI-Native Premium

**File:** [`sample-1-ai-native.html`](sample-1-ai-native.html)

**Vibe:** Anthropic.com / Linear / OpenAI. Dark base (near-black), warm cream typography, single amber-glow accent, large Fraunces serif headlines, subtle radial mesh in the background, hairline borders, no decoration.

**Palette:** Near-black `#0A0A0A` · cream `#F5F1EA` · amber accent `#D97706` · gold glow `#FCD34D`.

**Typography:** Inter for UI, Fraunces (serif) for display headlines. Massive 88px display with -4.5px letter-spacing for that "expensive AI product" feel.

**Best for:**
- Convincing a tech-aware audience the product is serious
- Demo videos and recruiter eyes
- Standing out from the rest of Indian EdTech (most of which is bright)
- LinkedIn/Twitter share images

**Trade-off:** Dark UI is fashionable but a smaller subset of coaching institute owners will instantly recognize it as a CRM. Doesn't telegraph "education" as obviously as the other directions.

## Sample 2 — Indian EdTech Bold

**File:** [`sample-2-bold-indian.html`](sample-2-bold-indian.html)

**Vibe:** PhysicsWallah / Allen / Vedantu confidence — but more design-forward. Saffron + maroon + gold palette. Bricolage Grotesque display font with high-contrast slabs. A bilingual Devanagari Hindi pull-quote ("मेहनत हम करते हैं। बाकी सब GrowthDesk।"). An auto-scrolling student-success ticker. Result cards with neo-brutalist shadows.

**Palette:** Cream `#FFF8F0` · ink `#0E0E1F` · saffron `#FF6B1A` · maroon `#7B0E1E` · gold `#F4B400`.

**Typography:** Bricolage Grotesque (display) · Plus Jakarta Sans (body) · Anek Devanagari (Hindi moments).

**Best for:**
- Selling to coaching institute owners who already trust the PhysicsWallah/Allen aesthetic
- An audience that responds to bold motivational copy
- Showing "this is built for India" at first glance
- Conversion-focused (higher click-through than minimalist designs in this market)

**Trade-off:** The aesthetic is strong — a sophisticated B2B buyer might find it loud. Also commits to the Hindi-English bilingual moment, which is great for warmth but limits portability if you ever target non-Hindi-speaking regions.

## Sample 3 — Duolingo-Style Friendly

**File:** [`sample-3-duolingo.html`](sample-3-duolingo.html)

**Vibe:** Duolingo / Kahoot / Cred. Bright primary green/blue/yellow, fully rounded everything (chunky 14–18px corner radii), button shadows that "click" on hover, streak/XP gamification visible front-and-center, cheerful. The hero shows a live diagnostic-quiz interaction with a 3-question streak indicator.

**Palette:** Off-white `#FFFDF7` · green `#58CC02` · blue `#1CB0F6` · pink `#FF6FAE` · yellow `#FFC800`.

**Typography:** Nunito (display + buttons, very rounded) · DM Sans (body).

**Best for:**
- Talking directly to JEE/CET students (the actual end users — age 16-18)
- Highlighting the gamification, streaks, badges, and adaptive learning features
- Capturing the daily-habit angle of JEE prep
- Looking different from competitors (most Indian EdTech CRMs are corporate-blue)

**Trade-off:** Slightly less "B2B credibility" with coaching institute owners who associate friendly UI with consumer apps. Best if your sales motion is bottom-up (students first, institutes follow).

## Sample 4 — Stripe-Style Editorial

**File:** [`sample-4-stripe-editorial.html`](sample-4-stripe-editorial.html)

**Vibe:** Stripe.com / Notion / Linear's marketing site. Pristine white + tinted-cream sections + a subtle indigo gradient that anchors the brand. Newsreader serif (italic) used for editorial moments — `built for the way India learns` with the middle italic. A realistic dashboard mock with KPIs and a sparkline chart. A pull-quote with avatar.

**Palette:** White `#FFFFFF` · ink `#0A2540` · primary indigo `#635BFF` · tinted-cream `#F4F2EE` · slate `#697384`.

**Typography:** Inter (UI) · Newsreader (editorial italic moments).

**Best for:**
- Owning the "premium B2B SaaS" frame in the EdTech CRM space
- Selling to institute owners who already buy software (familiar visual language → lower friction)
- A serious investor pitch deck or partnership conversation
- Long-term brand: this style ages well because it's restrained

**Trade-off:** Risk of looking too generic — the Stripe template has been copied so much that it can read as "yet another SaaS startup." The italic editorial moments and pull-quote help differentiate.

## How to pick

Open all four files in your browser. Spend 30 seconds with each. Notice which one you instinctively want to scroll past versus which one stops you. Then ask yourself:

- **Who's looking at this first?** Institute owner = #2 or #4. Student = #3. Tech-savvy investor = #1 or #4.
- **What's the unfair advantage?** "Built for India" = #2. "Built with AI" = #1. "Engaging for students" = #3. "Premium B2B" = #4.
- **Where will it get shared?** LinkedIn = #1 or #4. Coaching institute WhatsApp groups = #2. Student forums and Instagram = #3.
